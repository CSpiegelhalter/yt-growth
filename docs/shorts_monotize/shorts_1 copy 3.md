## Core Claim: Shorts Can “Print,” Especially With Music Deals
- Shorts earnings vary widely and can look low per view at first.

## Pros and Cons of Starting a Shorts Channel

### Pros
- Beginner-friendly: easier, faster, cheaper to produce than long-form.
- Lower skill barrier than long-form (you don’t need to become an expert editor/filmmaker to start).
- Faster potential “blow up” timeline:
  - With a good niche + concept + execution, growth can happen in ~1–2 months (varies by creator/channel).

### Cons
- Heavy competition:
  - Many creators replicate formats quickly (even “AI cloning” voices is mentioned).
- You must differentiate even when copying what works (the script suggests 5–10% variation).

## The Shorts Channel Playbook

## Step 1 — Pick a Niche (3 Methods)

### Method 1: Search YouTube Directly (If You Already Have a Direction)
- Look up the niche/topic.
- If creators are posting and pulling strong views:
  - That’s validation.
  - The recommended strategy: copy what works and make it **5–10% different**.

### Method 2: Use Leaderboard/Analytics Sites to Find Winning Niches
- Example mentioned: “ViewStats by MrBeast”
- Approach:
  - Filter/sort top channels by niche, time window, geography.
  - Find channels already “printing,” then model their structure.

### Method 3: “Doom Scroll” + Train Your Feed
- Train your Shorts/For You feed to show a specific content type.
- Pay attention to recurring patterns in what you personally get served.
- Take notes on what’s performing well, then build from those patterns.
- Example from the script:
  - A “celebrity stories” feed led to a niche choice for a channel (“Slam Dunk”).

---

## Step 2 — Build a “Checklist” From Top Channels
- Watch the best performers in your niche.
- Write down everything they do (even if you don’t like it).
- Assumption: top channels have repeatable patterns you don’t see yet.

### Example Checklist Items (From “Slam Dunk”)
- Eye-catching first clips
- High-quality video clips
- Avoid certain content categories (kissing / dangerous activity)
- Visual illustration matters
- Script structure:
  - Sentence 1 = hook (stop the scroll)
  - Sentence 2 = re-hook (promise momentum/keep them watching)
  - The first half of sentence 2 should feel like it’s “getting into it”
  - The second half of sentence 2 should function as a re-hook

---

## Step 3 — Make the Video (Skills + Workflow)

### Core Skills to Develop
- Research
- Scriptwriting
- Voiceover
- Editing

### Editing Strategy
- Beginners: edit yourself at first to build fundamentals.
- Alternative path:
  - Hire/partner with an editor (the script mentions finding editors on Twitter and a site like “ytjobs.com”).
  - Build relationships and collaborate.

### Example: “Zoink and Twist” (Copy + Improve)
- Process described:
  1. Identify a proven concept (e.g., “MrBeast’s first subscriber” video format).
  2. Re-make it with a new subject (e.g., “Kai Cenat’s first subscriber”).
  3. Improve pacing and hook delivery.
  4. Use payoff clips to prevent drop-off near the end.

### Script/Pacing Notes From the Walkthrough
- If delivery is slow early on, viewers scroll.
- Make the 2nd sentence faster, punchier, and funnier.
- Use transition words like “’cause” (as described) to make it feel like you’re getting into the story.
- Re-hook idea:
  - Give a small “sauce” reveal, then say something like “but none of them were first” to keep curiosity high.
- End payoff:
  - Reveal the answer + show a clip that feels like a satisfying conclusion.

### Production Timing (Claimed)
- Once skilled:
  - Script + voiceover can take **15–30 minutes**
- Early stage:
  - Don’t rush; use the checklist.

## Step 4 — Understand Analytics (The Viral Feedback Loop)

### The 3 Metrics to Watch
1. **Swipe rate** (early drop-off / swiping away)
2. **Average percentage viewed**
3. **Retention graph shape**

### Interpretation (As Described)
- Low swipes in the first ~4 seconds is good.
- High average percentage viewed is a strong signal (the script cites ~98% as “really good” for the niche).
- A flatter retention graph is ideal.
- A big drop indicates boredom/confusion at a specific moment:
  - Identify the cause and fix it in the next video.

### The Iteration Cycle (Core Operating System)
1. Post a video
2. Review analytics
3. Identify 1 improvement
4. Apply it to the next video
5. Repeat
- Expectation-setting:
  - Even experienced creators post many videos that get little traction.
  - Some early “zero-view” videos can later gain millions once the channel has momentum.
