# YouTube Upload Sequence That YouTube Rewards (and the One That Kills Videos)

## Core Claim: YouTube Doesn’t Hate Small Creators — It Hates Sloppy Uploads
- There’s an upload sequence that YouTube “rewards,” and another that can suppress your video before it gets a fair chance.
- The speaker frames this as learned experience after years of inconsistent results and blaming everything except the upload process.
- Key takeaway: the “poison” isn’t the content itself — it’s publishing before the metadata and packaging are ready.

---

## The Root Problem: “Metadata Poisoning”
### Definition
**Metadata poisoning** = publishing a video while key metadata is incomplete or low-quality (title not final, thumbnail unfinished, description empty, etc.), causing YouTube’s earliest tests to run on “garbage” inputs.

### Typical Scenario
- You’re behind schedule.
- You publish to hit a self-imposed deadline.
- You plan to “fix it in a few minutes.”

### Why This Hurts
- The moment you go **public**, YouTube starts testing:
  - Title + thumbnail to a small audience (CTR test)
  - Description + metadata to understand context and audience targeting
- The first impression data gets collected immediately.
- Later edits can help, but **you’re not starting fresh**—you’re correcting damage after the initial test already ran.

---

## Important Clarification: Uploading Early Is Good — Publishing Early Is Bad
### Good Practice
- Upload 1–2+ days early.
- Keep the video **private or unlisted** while it processes.
- Use the time to triple-check everything before it goes public.

### Bad Practice
- Uploading 15 minutes before go-live, stressed, and missing key steps.
- Going public before everything is locked.

---

# The “Locked-In Before Public” Upload Sequence

## 1) Title Must Be Final (Before You Publish)
### Why It Matters
- Title is a primary signal for:
  - what the video is about
  - which searches it appears in
  - which audiences it’s tested on first
  - how YouTube categorizes the video

### Rule
- If you’re still workshopping the title when you publish, you’re already behind.

> Note: The speaker also claims the title should ideally be decided before recording (but treats that as a separate topic).

---

## 2) Thumbnail Must Be Final (Before You Publish)
### Why It Matters
- If you publish without a real thumbnail:
  - YouTube tests your video with an autogenerated thumbnail
  - People don’t click
  - That early CTR data becomes part of the video’s performance history

### Rule
- Thumbnail should be designed, exported, and uploaded before going public.

### Extra Detail: File Names (Optional Micro-Optimization)
- Speaker recommendation: rename thumbnail/video files to match the video title.
- Acknowledges debate about whether filenames matter, but argues it’s trivial effort.

---

## 3) Description Matters More Than People Think
### Why It Matters
- YouTube uses the description to understand:
  - context
  - related topics
  - intent beyond the transcript

### Emphasis: First 150 Characters
- The first ~150 characters matter most because that’s what appears in search previews.

### How to Write It
- Write as if explaining the video to someone who can’t watch it.
- Transcript = words said; description = intention and context.

### Workflow Tip: Upload Defaults
- In YouTube Studio:
  - Settings → Upload defaults
  - Save your standard links/offers/outro blocks once
  - Then each upload auto-fills those parts
  - You only write the unique content each time

---

## 4) Playlists Should Be Set Before Publishing
### Why It Matters
- Playlists signal which videos belong together and help YouTube group/categorize content.
- Adding a video to playlists **before** it’s public helps YouTube factor that association into early recommendation logic.

### Rule
- Add the video to playlists right before publishing, not after.

---

## 5) Chapters: Avoid Auto Chapters, Create Intentional Ones
### Claim
- YouTube autogenerated chapters are “garbage” because they produce vague, non-searchable chapter titles (e.g., “discussion,” “continuation”).

### Why It Matters
- Chapter titles can be indexed by Google.
- Good chapter titles can match search queries and send viewers directly to relevant timestamps (“free traffic”).

### Recommendation
- Don’t use auto chapters.
- Write chapters manually, or use AI tools to assist (ChatGPT/Gemini mentioned), but manual is preferred.

---

## 6) End Screens: Choose the Next Step Intentionally
### Goal
- Build “session time” (viewer goes from one of your videos to another).

### How to Choose the End Screen Video
- Not necessarily the newest upload.
- Choose the video that logically continues the viewer’s journey based on what they just watched.

### Principle
- End screens aren’t optional polish — they’re a deliberate redirect inside your ecosystem.

# Timing & Publishing Strategy

## Best Time to Upload: Consistency Helps, But Weekly Output Matters More (Early On)
### Early Channel Stage
- Posting at the exact same day/time is less important than publishing consistently (e.g., weekly).
- Missing weeks to maintain a strict schedule is framed as worse than shifting the day.

### Later Stage (Once You Have an Audience)
- A strict schedule can build loyalty faster.
- That becomes the longer-term goal.

---

## Use Audience Activity Data for Timing
- Use tools like:
  - VidIQ / TubeBuddy
  - YouTube Studio audience activity indicators
- Recognize the data may take several uploads to become reliable.
- Timing usually won’t “make or break” a video, but publishing when your audience is online is still a positive edge.

---

## Scheduling vs Manual Publish: Scheduling Is Fine, But Don’t Miss the First Hour
### The Common Scheduling Mistake
- Creators schedule a video, then aren’t present when it goes live.
- They don’t respond to comments early.

### The “First Hour” Importance (Especially Under ~50k Subs)
- Being active during the first hour is framed as a meaningful advantage.
- Recommendation: set a phone reminder so you’re available.

# What YouTube Measures Early (and Why the First Hour Is Crucial)

## 1) Click-Through Rate (CTR)
### How It Works
- YouTube shows thumbnail/title to a test audience.
- Measures clicks vs impressions.
- High CTR → expands distribution.
- Low CTR → holds back the video.

### Non-Negotiable
- Title + thumbnail must be locked before publishing because the first test starts immediately.

---

## 2) Watch Time & Retention Curve
### Core Point
- YouTube doesn’t need thousands of views to evaluate retention.
- Early viewer behavior becomes predictive.

### Example Mechanism
- If early viewers consistently drop at a specific point (e.g., ~27 seconds), YouTube assumes broader audiences will too.

### Framing
- Retention = trust signal.

---

## 3) Engagement Velocity (Likes, Comments, Shares)
### Why It Matters
- Engagement signals more than passive views; it shows the video provokes reaction.

### Practical Tactic: Pinned Comment Question
- Speaker pins a comment on every video.
- Uses a question to prompt replies and interaction.
- Claims high pinned-comment engagement can contribute to stronger distribution signals.

---

## 4) End-of-Video Behavior (Often Missed)
### What YouTube Tracks
- Do viewers:
  - click another one of your videos (strong positive signal)
  - leave the platform (negative)
  - go watch a competitor (also noticed)

### Takeaway
- End screen strategy influences whether viewers stay in your ecosystem.

---

# Summary: YouTube Upload Checklist (Repeat Every Upload)

## Pre-Publish “Locked In” Checklist
- **Title** finalized and intentional
- **Thumbnail** finalized and uploaded (no autogenerated placeholder tests)
- **Description** written with strong first ~150 characters + full context
- **Playlists** selected before going public
- **Chapters** written intentionally (avoid auto chapters)
- **End screens** chosen to continue the viewer journey

## Launch & First Hour Checklist
- Publish when audience is likely active (if you have data)
- Be present during the first hour:
  - respond to comments
  - encourage engagement (pinned question comment)
- Monitor early signals:
  - CTR
  - retention curve
  - engagement velocity
- Ensure end screens push viewers to another relevant video

---

## Closing Message Framing (Motivation)
- Treat the process as a repeatable checklist.
- Stop “killing” videos with sloppy uploads.
- Focus on intentional packaging + first-hour execution.
